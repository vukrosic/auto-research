# Lab Charter

## Mission

Autoresearch exists to operate like a small, high-tempo AI research lab:

- generate testable ideas
- run them cheaply and quickly
- promote only validated improvements
- compound knowledge instead of repeating work

## What The Lab Optimizes For

- Research velocity
- Decision quality
- Reproducibility
- Clear ownership
- Efficient use of compute

## What Counts As Success

- The current best system improves over time on a trusted metric
- Every experiment leaves usable knowledge behind
- Promotions are traceable and defensible
- Idle GPU time stays low
- The lab can be resumed from markdown state without hidden context

## Lab Principles

1. One project, one source of metric truth.
2. Every experiment must answer a specific question.
3. Baselines are first-class citizens, not background assumptions.
4. Promotions require evidence, not enthusiasm.
5. Failures are assets only if written down clearly.
6. Rules that are not written do not exist.

## Scope

This lab handbook defines the operating model for all projects run inside `autoresearch`.
Project-specific details belong under `projects/`, `knowledge/`, and `experiments/`.

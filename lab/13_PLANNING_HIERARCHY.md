# Planning Hierarchy

Policy version: 1.0
Effective: 2026-03-25

## Purpose

Defines the multi-timeframe planning cascade that lets an AI agent autonomously run a year-long research program. Each level sets context for the level below. Results flow up; plans flow down.

## The Five Levels

```
MISSION  (1 year)   — Human sets. What to achieve.
  ↓
YEAR PLAN           — AI generates. Strategic roadmap.
  ↓
QUARTER PLAN        — AI generates. Phased execution with milestones.
  ↓
MONTH PLAN          — AI generates. Campaigns and resource allocation.
  ↓
WEEK PLAN           — AI generates. Concrete experiments and GPU schedule.
  ↓
WAVE                — AI generates. Batch of experiments within a week.
```

## Level Definitions

### Mission (Human-Written)

**File**: `goals/<goal>/MISSION.md`
**Set by**: Human
**Duration**: 1 year
**Reviewed**: Annually by human (can be revised at any time by human)

The mission is the north star. It defines:
- The measurable objective (e.g., "beat X metric by Y amount")
- The scope (which projects, which codebases)
- Hard constraints (budget ceiling, deadline, hardware)
- What success looks like at the end of the year

> **RULE: The AI never modifies MISSION.md.** It can suggest changes to the human, but the mission is human-owned. Everything else cascades from it.

### Year Plan (AI-Generated)

**File**: `goals/<goal>/plans/year.md`
**Generated by**: AI, at goal creation
**Duration**: 1 year (aligned with mission)
**Reviewed**: Every quarter boundary (or after major pivot)

The year plan is the strategic roadmap. It contains:
- **Phase breakdown**: What to focus on each quarter (e.g., Q1: architecture exploration, Q2: scaling and optimization, Q3: combination and hardening, Q4: final push)
- **Milestone targets**: Expected metric at each quarter boundary
- **Risk assessment**: What could block progress, contingency plans
- **Resource assumptions**: Expected GPU access over the year
- **Research axes inventory**: All known directions to explore, prioritized

> **REVIEW RULE**: Review the year plan when a quarter ends. Do not review mid-quarter unless a major pivot trigger fires (see 11_RESEARCH_STRATEGY.md). A year plan reviewed too often becomes a week plan.

**Minimum data before review**: 1 full quarter of execution data (experiments run, metrics collected, knowledge generated).

### Quarter Plan (AI-Generated)

**File**: `goals/<goal>/plans/q<N>_<YYYY>.md`
**Generated by**: AI, at quarter start (or when the previous quarter plan expires)
**Duration**: 3 months
**Reviewed**: Every month boundary

The quarter plan translates the year plan's phase into concrete campaigns:
- **Active campaigns**: Which research directions to pursue
- **Resource allocation**: GPU-hours per campaign
- **Milestone**: Target metric by quarter end
- **Gate criteria**: What must be true to proceed to next quarter's phase

> **REVIEW RULE**: Review at month boundary. Update campaign priorities based on month results. Do not start new campaigns mid-month unless a pivot trigger fires.

**Minimum data before review**: 1 full month of execution data.

### Month Plan (AI-Generated)

**File**: `goals/<goal>/plans/<YYYY>_<MM>.md`
**Generated by**: AI, at month start
**Duration**: 1 month
**Reviewed**: Every week boundary

The month plan allocates resources to campaigns and sets weekly targets:
- **Campaign schedule**: Which campaigns run which weeks
- **GPU allocation**: Hours per week per campaign
- **Weekly targets**: Expected experiments and metric milestones
- **Budget tracking**: Spend rate, runway

> **REVIEW RULE**: Review at week boundary. Reallocate hours between campaigns if one is outperforming or underperforming. Do not redesign campaigns mid-week.

**Minimum data before review**: 1 full week of execution data.

### Week Plan (AI-Generated)

**File**: `goals/<goal>/plans/<YYYY>_w<WW>.md`
**Generated by**: AI, at week start
**Duration**: 1 week
**Reviewed**: Daily (at session open)

The week plan is the execution schedule:
- **GPU schedule**: What runs when, on which GPU
- **Wave definitions**: Batches of experiments with axes and expected durations
- **Decision points**: When to review and potentially pivot
- **Capacity calculations**: Exact run counts with math shown
- **Compute ledger**: Updated after each experiment

> **REVIEW RULE**: Review daily at session open. Update the wave queue based on yesterday's results. The GPU must never be idle.

### Wave (AI-Generated, Ephemeral)

Waves are batches of 8-15 experiments within a week plan. They don't get their own file — they're sections within the week plan. A wave is:
- Designed after adjudicating the previous wave
- Executed over hours to days
- Adjudicated immediately upon completion

## Cascade Rules

### Downward Flow (Plans → Execution)

1. Each level must be consistent with the level above it
2. A week plan cannot pursue a campaign not listed in the month plan
3. A month plan cannot start a campaign not listed in the quarter plan
4. A quarter plan cannot pursue a direction not in the year plan
5. **Exception**: Opportunistic discoveries can violate the hierarchy. If an experiment reveals something unexpected and promising, the agent can pursue it for ONE wave, then must update the plans above to accommodate or reject it.

### Upward Flow (Results → Plans)

1. Wave results update the week plan (daily)
2. Week results update the month plan (weekly)
3. Month results update the quarter plan (monthly)
4. Quarter results update the year plan (quarterly)
5. Results NEVER update the mission (only human can)

### Update Protocol

When updating a plan at any level:

1. **Append, don't overwrite.** Add an "## Updates" section at the bottom. The original plan stays for audit.
2. **State what changed and why.** "Pivoting from axis X to axis Y because [evidence]."
3. **Check consistency upward.** Does this update conflict with the level above? If so, update that level too.
4. **Check consistency downward.** Does this update invalidate plans below? If so, regenerate them.

### Anti-Thrashing Rule

> **HARD RULE: Do not update a plan level more frequently than its review cadence.**
>
> - Year plan: max 4 updates/year (quarterly)
> - Quarter plan: max 3 updates/quarter (monthly)
> - Month plan: max 5 updates/month (weekly + 1 emergency)
> - Week plan: max 7 updates/week (daily)
>
> If you feel the urge to update more often, the plan at the level above is wrong. Fix that instead.

## Review Protocol

At each review boundary, answer these questions:

1. **Progress**: Are we on track vs the milestone at this level?
2. **Velocity**: Is improvement rate sufficient to hit the next milestone?
3. **Spend**: Is resource consumption on budget?
4. **Surprises**: Did anything unexpected happen that changes the strategy?
5. **Next period**: What's the plan for the next period at this level?

Compute **velocity** as:

```
velocity = (metric_at_period_start - metric_now) / gpu_hours_spent
```

If velocity is declining for 2+ periods at the same level → pivot trigger. See 11_RESEARCH_STRATEGY.md.

## Goal Modularity

### Multiple Concurrent Goals

The system supports multiple active goals. Each goal is fully self-contained under `goals/<goal>/`:

```
goals/
  surpass_transformer_2026/     # Goal 1
    MISSION.md
    plans/
    campaigns/
    progress.md
    resources.md
  efficient_inference_2026/     # Goal 2 (hypothetical)
    MISSION.md
    plans/
    campaigns/
    progress.md
    resources.md
```

### Goal Isolation

- Goals do NOT share campaigns or plans
- Goals CAN share GPU resources (allocated via resources.md at each goal level)
- Goals CAN share knowledge (cross-goal findings go in `knowledge/meta/`)
- Goals CAN share projects (parameter-golf codebase can serve multiple goals)
- Goals CAN share experiments (an experiment's goal is tagged in meta.json)

### Shared Resources

When multiple goals compete for the same GPU:
1. Check each goal's `resources.md` for allocated hours
2. Dispatch from the goal whose allocation has the most headroom
3. If equal, dispatch from the goal with highest velocity (most improvement per hour)
4. Never let one goal starve another for >1 week

## Agent Continuity

> **CRITICAL: This system must survive agent handoff.** If a new AI agent (or new conversation) takes over, they must be able to read the files and continue without human explanation.

For this to work:
1. All state is in files, never in conversation memory alone
2. Plans explicitly state "what to do next" — not just "what was done"
3. Progress.md in each goal tracks the metric timeline with dates
4. Every plan update includes the reasoning, not just the decision
5. Knowledge files are authoritative — they represent the lab's accumulated understanding

### Agent Onboarding Checklist

A new agent starting a session should:
1. Read `AGENTS.md` (system overview)
2. Read `LAB.md` (policy index)
3. For each active goal: read `MISSION.md` → current quarter plan → current month plan → current week plan
4. Check running experiments
5. Resume from wherever the week plan says to resume
